Focus on us: Dallas 2019, by The Dallas Morning News

build awareness. shine light. impact lives.

    What issues are impacting us, all of us? Directly, indirectly, emotionally, civically? Issues that can stir emotion and have the potential to divide — sex trafficking, teaching kids in poverty and civic engagement. Issues that aren’t always easy to talk about, but talking about them is exactly what we’re going to do — with a goal to understand and to unite.

    Our series, Focus On Us is going to tackle the issues that deserve awareness, need understanding and can ultimately change policy, impact lives and make a stronger Dallas for all of us.

    sex trafficking

    build awareness.

    rescue lives.

    You see them sometimes in the shadows, sometimes in the streets, sometimes on the sidewalks in parts of the city many of us don’t go. Sometimes, too often, we don’t see them at all. Across our city and region, thousands of people are trapped in sexual slavery, sold for their bodies in circumstances they cannot escape. We need to face their struggle and to answer their powerlessness with our power.

    We can expose the truth about the sex trade. We can punish the traffickers for stealing someone’s life and selling it. We can bring the victims back into freedom and fullness.

      teaching in poverty

      build awareness.
      open doors.
      grow lives.

      There are few challenges in education as great as teaching impoverished children. Many come to school hungry and will wake up hungry again the next day. But we face no more important job than ensuring these children are educated.

      We are getting better at understanding how to do this: high-quality pre-kindergarten, paying our best teachers the best salaries, encouraging them to work in our toughest schools and increasing the state’s support for the classroom. The work is just beginning, but it is the best work we can do.

        civic engagement

        Let’s talk about where we live, Dallas. Let’s talk about how important it is to us. Here’s the hard truth. We say it’s important, but when it comes to voting in local elections, Dallas is dead last among America’s 50 largest cities. Dead last. We can do better, and it starts with understanding the place where we live.

        The issues, the concerns, the things that shape Dallas: those are the things you need to know. So you can understand. So you can act.
